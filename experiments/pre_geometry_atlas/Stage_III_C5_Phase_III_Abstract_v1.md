# Stage III C5 -- Phase III Abstract v1

Phase III on the frozen HAOS (Harmonic Address Operating System) / IIP (Interaction Invariance Physics) Dirac-Kahler collision branch resolves a narrow clustered interaction sector rather than a family-wide braid mechanism. Mechanism-isolation, reduction, and closure tests show that braid-like exchange does not generalize beyond clustered seeds, while the surviving clustered braid/smear sector admits a compact two-state surrogate and closes only as a one-sided smeared-transfer effective phase map. No stable braid phase or localized encounter phase survives the full closure criteria. Within the clustered sector, the braid/smear split is controlled primarily by the phase corridor, with geometry overlap and `0 <-> 1` operator cross-coupling acting as secondary supports, while grade transfer does not emerge as the primary driver on the frozen branch. The mature Phase III result is therefore a bounded phenomenological closure: a finite smeared-transfer sector survives, whereas braid remains a transient clustered texture-level descriptor rather than a stable intrinsic mechanism.
