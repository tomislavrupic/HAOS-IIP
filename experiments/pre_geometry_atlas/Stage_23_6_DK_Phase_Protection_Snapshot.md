# Stage 23.6 DK Phase Protection Snapshot

Stage 23.6 tested whether the clustered Dirac-Kahler braid-like exchange family identified in Stage 23.5 is robust across a finite phase set or collapses under phase detuning.

## Frozen result

- total runs: 9
- topology labels: 8 braid-like exchange, 1 transfer-smeared
- braid corridor: baseline, quarter offset, three-quarter offset, anti-phase
- skew perturbations preserved braid-like exchange
- weak beta modulation preserved braid-like exchange

## Why this counts as an exploratory positive

- braid-like exchange survives across a finite phase corridor rather than at a single tuned point
- collapse is localized to a smeared midpoint window, not monotonic across phase detuning
- the phase response therefore reflects internal DK collision structure, not trivial tuning noise

## Clean conclusion

Dirac-Kahler clustered encounters exhibit a reproducible braid-like exchange topology that is robust across a finite phase corridor, with a localized smeared midpoint window rather than monotonic phase collapse.
