# Stage 21 Negative Snapshot

Stage 21 tested whether bounded registration-gated interaction eligibility could create persistence without introducing substrate memory.

Registration classes tested:
- `phase_coherence`
- `spatial_configuration`
- `composite_topology`

Representative set:
- `clustered_composite_anchor`
- `phase_ordered_symmetric_triad`
- `counter_propagating_corridor`

Base result:
- `27/27` runs returned `no registration effect`
- no composite lifetime gain
- no binding persistence gain
- no coarse basin persistence gain
- no corridor dwell gain or transport locking
- no promoted `12 -> 24` follow-up

Why this still counts as a real probe:
- non-null runs carried bounded gated interaction energy
- spatial and topology registration both produced nonzero duty cycles
- registration windows existed, but no persistence metric moved

Representative activation scale:
- `phase_coherence`
  - max mean registration strength: `0.1829`
  - max effective interaction energy fraction: `0.0033`
  - duty cycle remained `0.0000`
- `spatial_configuration`
  - max mean registration strength: `0.5519`
  - max effective interaction energy fraction: `0.0134`
  - duty cycle reached `1.0000`
- `composite_topology`
  - max mean registration strength: `0.3119`
  - max effective interaction energy fraction: `0.0073`
  - duty cycle reached `1.0000`

Core conclusion:

Mutual registration and constraint-coupled interaction eligibility are insufficient, at the tested couplings, to improve composite lifetime, binding persistence, coarse basin persistence, or corridor locking in the current architecture.

Interpretation boundary:
- this falsifies selective interaction gating as a weak persistence mechanism in the tested bounded class
- it does not support emergent geometry, relational capture, or interaction-law closure
- the next coherent step is a genuinely new degree of freedom or constraint class, not another weak gating variant
