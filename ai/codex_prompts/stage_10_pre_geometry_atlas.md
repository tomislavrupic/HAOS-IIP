ATLAS PASSES

Implement the framework to support four passes.

PASS A — Baseline morphology

Goal: determine what the untouched architecture naturally does.

Use:
- regular graph
- default kernel
- single packet
- no DK dynamics yet
- three packet widths
- three central frequencies
- two amplitudes
- two boundary types

Questions:
- Which modes travel cleanly?
- Which disperse immediately?
- Which remain trapped?
- Does coherence prefer low-k, mid-k, or high-k?
- Do boundaries dominate the result?

PASS B — Robustness and perturbation

Goal: determine what survives mild disorder.

Change one perturbation at a time:
- small edge-weight perturbation
- small graph defect density
- small anisotropic bias
- slight kernel-width variation

Questions:
- What is structurally stable?
- What breaks immediately?
- Which sectors remain recoverable?
- Does the transverse band remain privileged?

PASS C — Spectral morphology

Goal: explore mode-space before position-space interpretation dominates.

Use:
- pure spectral seeds
- phase-shifted twin packets
- broadband packets
- narrowband packets
- deliberately mismatched phase envelopes

Questions:
- Do some spectral combinations self-organize into cleaner transport?
- Do phase relations generate persistent structure?
- Do interference patterns create quasi-stable corridors or nodes?
- Does the system prefer certain bands?

PASS D — Proto-collective behavior

Goal: test whether multiple excitations produce pre-geometric order.

Use:
- two packets
- three packets
- symmetric and asymmetric injection
- different phase offsets
- later weak DK coupling if ready

Questions:
- Do packets pass through?
- Lock?
- Scatter?
- Merge?
- Create standing structures?
- Form rotating or synchronized patterns?
