PROJECT: STAGE 11 — COLLECTIVE WAVE INTERACTION ATLAS
REPO CONTEXT: HAOS/IIP frozen architecture
MODE: multi-packet interaction atlas
PRIORITY: interaction morphology before interpretation

Purpose

Extend the Pre-Geometry Atlas from single-packet dynamics to multi-packet interaction regimes.
Goal is to classify whether stable large-scale curvature-like or attraction-like behaviour emerges from purely local kernel-induced dynamics.

Core philosophy

No kernel definition, weighting rule, or cochain operator construction may be changed.

This stage studies the dynamical behaviour of multiple interacting wave packets on fixed graph complexes.

The objective is classification, not interpretation.

Axes to vary (one at a time unless explicitly testing coupling)

Packet ensemble structure
	•	number of packets
	•	initial separation distance
	•	relative phase offset
	•	amplitude ratios
	•	coherent vs incoherent packet families
	•	symmetric vs asymmetric spatial seeding

Interaction regime
	•	weak overlap
	•	intermittent overlap
	•	sustained overlap
	•	clustered packet groups
	•	chain-like propagation configurations

Graph background
	•	homogeneous lattice
	•	perturbed connectivity
	•	multi-scale clustered regions
	•	anisotropic connectivity corridors

Observables

Track and store:
	•	packet centroid trajectories
	•	persistence length of coherent envelopes
	•	phase-locking events
	•	merger / repulsion / orbit-like patterns
	•	lifetime of composite structures
	•	energy-like norm redistribution

Atlas classification outputs

Each run must be labelled only in descriptive terms:
	•	dispersive independent regime
	•	transient binding regime
	•	metastable composite regime
	•	oscillatory exchange regime
	•	large-scale drift field regime

Do not assign physical interpretation.

Do not use words such as gravity, mass, curvature, force.

The goal is to map interaction morphology.
