Stage 9B Rationale

Dirac–Kähler Wave-Packet Dynamics

Position in the Program

The previous stages established the static operator structure of the kernel-induced cochain architecture.

Key results already validated:

Paper 1–2
- scalar Laplacian emergence
- restricted transverse spectral band
- continuum-like scaling behavior

Paper 3
- Dirac–Kähler factorization

D_{DK} = d + \delta

with numerical verification that

D_{DK}^2 = \Delta_H

across the graded cochain complex.

Paper 4

- longitudinal decoupling
- discrete Gauss-law relation
- structured loop observables

These results establish that the architecture supports multiple operator sectors derived from the same Gaussian kernel substrate.

However, all previous results are spectral or algebraic.

They do not yet test dynamic behavior.

Why Dynamics Must Be Tested

Spectral agreement alone is insufficient to determine whether an operator system behaves like a physical propagation system.

Many discrete operators exhibit:
- clean spectra
- algebraic identities
- symmetry relations

but still fail to support coherent propagation when evolved dynamically.

The next logical step in the program is therefore:

test packet propagation under the validated operators.

Stage 9 introduces this for the scalar and transverse sectors.

Stage 9B extends the same idea to the Dirac–Kähler sector.

Why the Dirac–Kähler Sector Matters

The Dirac–Kähler operator

D_{DK} = d + \delta

is a first-order operator whose square produces the Hodge Laplacian:

D_{DK}^2 = \Delta_H

This structure is mathematically analogous to how Dirac operators generate relativistic propagation in continuum field theory.

In lattice geometry, Dirac–Kähler formulations are known to encode fermion-like structures using differential forms rather than spinors.

However, in the present architecture:
- no fermions were imposed
- no spinor discretization was introduced
- the operator arises directly from the cochain complex.

Stage 9B therefore tests a purely structural question:

does this validated first-order operator produce coherent packet propagation when used as a dynamical generator?

What Stage 9B Tests

Stage 9B focuses on operator-level dynamics.

Specifically it examines:
1. first-order DK packet propagation

\partial_t \Psi = i D_{DK} \Psi

2. consistency with second-order evolution

\partial_t^2 \Psi = - \Delta_H \Psi

3. grade-resolved packet evolution

tracking how energy distributes across

\Omega^0, \Omega^1, \Omega^2, \Omega^3

4. stability under flat holonomy conditions

extending the spectral tests of Stage 6–7.

What Counts as Success

Evidence of coherent propagation would include:
- stable packet motion
- controlled spreading
- norm preservation
- consistent grade mixing
- agreement between first-order DK evolution and induced second-order dynamics.

Such behavior would demonstrate that the Dirac–Kähler operator is not merely a spectral artifact but can act as a dynamical generator on the architecture.

Interpretation Boundary

Stage 9B remains strictly within operator-level dynamics.

The results do not establish:
- fermions
- electrons
- quantum field theory
- particle interpretation
- relativistic invariance.

The stage only addresses the question:

does the validated Dirac–Kähler operator support coherent dynamical propagation on the frozen architecture?
