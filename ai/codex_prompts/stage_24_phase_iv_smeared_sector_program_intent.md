# Phase IV -- Effective-Sector Transport, Invariants, and Extension Discipline

Context:
HAOS = Harmonic Address Operating System.
IIP = Interaction Invariance Physics.

Purpose

Phase IV is a new branch built from the frozen Phase III ledger, not a continuation of the older braid search.

Phase III already closed the clustered Dirac-Kahler branch honestly:
- surviving positive core:
  - a clustered-sector two-state effective surrogate,
  - a one-sided smeared-transfer closed regime,
  - a mechanism read in which phase corridor is primary, with geometry overlap and `0 <-> 1` operator cross-coupling as secondary supports;
- frozen negatives:
  - no family-wide braid mechanism,
  - no stable braid phase,
  - no localized encounter phase,
  - no grade-transfer-primary claim.

Phase IV therefore treats the smeared-transfer sector, not braid, as the surviving positive object of the frozen DK branch.

Core intention

Take the surviving smeared regime seriously as the object and ask:
- what operational observables define it,
- what smallest effective law describes it,
- whether any invariant or quasi-invariant structure survives across it,
- and how far that effective content remains valid under bounded extension without changing operator class.

Interpretation discipline

Do not re-open Phase III by stealth.
Do not use Phase IV to retry family-wide braid claims.
Do not inflate clustered texture into ontology.
Do not treat standard-physics correspondence as a substitute for branch-local structure.

Phase IV remains:
- pre-foundational,
- branch-bounded,
- operator-class frozen,
- clustered-sector centered,
- and reduction-first.

The job is to make the surviving smeared sector more exact, not more grand.

Phase IV bounded goals

## IV-A -- Observable definition

Define the minimal operational observable basis of the surviving smeared sector.

Target quantities include:
- phase-corridor width,
- topology survival time,
- transfer asymmetry,
- refinement stability,
- reverse stability,
- coherence decay,
- and any branch-internal transport score that truly survives closure.

Question:
what are the minimal quantities that characterize the closed smeared regime?

## IV-B -- Effective law extraction

Write the smallest reduced rule-set or effective transport law for the smeared sector.

The goal is not a full theory.
The goal is a compact law that predicts regime membership and local persistence structure inside the surviving smeared corridor.

Question:
can the surviving sector be written as a compact transport law instead of just a phase map?

## IV-C -- Invariant / quasi-invariant probe

Look for quantities that remain stable across the allowed smeared region.

This is the first point at which the branch becomes more physics-shaped, but still without overclaiming.

Question:
does the closed smeared regime carry any branch-stable invariant, quasi-invariant, or conservation-like quantity?

## IV-D -- Extension boundary test

Only after IV-A through IV-C are complete, test whether the smeared law survives bounded structural extension.

The point is not to recover braid.
The point is to determine how far the smeared effective law has portability before operator class changes would be required.

Question:
what is the first radius beyond the clustered anchor where the smeared effective law still holds?
