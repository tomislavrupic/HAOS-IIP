# Stage 12 — Intrinsic Grading and Structural Irreversibility Probe

Purpose

Stage 12 is the austerity closure probe for the no-distance line.
It removes explicit harmonic-address weighting from the evolution rule and asks whether:

- intrinsic grading can be recovered from the dynamics alone
- structural irreversibility survives bidirectional cycling
- protection generalizes across clustered, corridor, and triad families
- mismatch-ledger orientation and signed-field evolution remain coupled

Hard constraints

- no coordinate embedding
- no Euclidean metric
- no Gaussian kernel
- no explicit harmonic-address labels in the evolution rule
- no new mediator field
- no new DK-sector lift

Allowed

- latent seed initialization using previously known compatible families
- post hoc recovered grading analysis
- forward / reverse / re-perturb schedules
- refinement doubling
- common family comparison with identical rule sets

Run lattice

- 3 families: clustered, corridor, triad
- 3 protocols: forward only, forward/reverse/restore, forward/reverse/re-perturb
- 2 resolutions: base, refined

Total core runs: 18

Internal sweep

Each run must internally sweep:

- latent detuning ladder
- protection strength ladder
- kernel-radius ladder

without introducing explicit selector dressing into the operator.

Recovered grading block

Recover sector structure from the dynamics alone using a post hoc spectral/coherence partition.
Track:

Closure logic

- Positive structural closure:
  intrinsic grading, family-wide recovery, orientation/evolution coupling, and irreversibility all survive
- Split closure:
  orientation/evolution and irreversibility survive, but intrinsic grading and family-wide recovery fail
- Negative closure:
  the strengthened obligations collapse under label-free cycling

Interpretation boundary

At most claim:

the combinatorial austerity line does or does not close under label-free grading recovery, bidirectional irreversibility, and family-wide protection tests.
