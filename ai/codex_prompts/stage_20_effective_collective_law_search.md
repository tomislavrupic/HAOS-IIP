Stage 20 — Effective Collective Law Search

(Positive-path diagnostic layer)

Position in the program

Stages 14–19 established a strong structural boundary:
	•	weak local extensions → no binding
	•	relational ordering → weak / context-dependent
	•	feedback reinjection → inert
	•	synchronization → absent
	•	substrate reinforcement → bounded but regime-inert

Therefore:

If a persistent large-scale ordering law exists in this architecture,
it must arise from collective effective dynamics,
not from incremental strengthening of previously tested couplings.

Stage 20 begins a controlled search for such a law.

⸻

Core question

Can a low-dimensional effective organizing dynamic be inferred from ensemble morphology evolution that:
	•	improves persistence measures
	•	survives resolution refinement
	•	does not require fine-tuned coefficients
	•	and is selective (case-dependent), not global?

This stage does not assume geometry, spacetime, or binding.
It searches only for repeatable collective regulation patterns.

⸻

Guiding principle

Treat the full frozen architecture as a data-generating system.
Then ask:

Is there a simple coarse dynamical law that predicts or stabilizes
regime-level ordering across cases?

⸻

Effective observable layer

Construct time-series observables per run:
	•	composite count
	•	composite lifetime distribution
	•	binding persistence
	•	coarse basin occupancy
	•	transport corridor activity
	•	phase-variance proxy
	•	flux anisotropy measures

From these derive:
	•	collective order parameter candidates
	•	persistence gradients
	•	transition likelihood maps

⸻

Candidate effective law families

Stage 20 explores three minimal classes.

1. Persistence-gradient regulation
Hypothesis:
System evolution contains an implicit drift toward states of higher basin residence.

Test:
	•	estimate local persistence gradient in observable space
	•	introduce weak global regulation term aligned with that gradient
	•	check whether persistence metrics improve selectively

2. Flux-balancing regulation
Hypothesis:
Instability arises from sustained transport imbalance.

Test:
	•	construct smoothed net-flux imbalance signal
	•	add weak corrective damping toward flux symmetry
	•	evaluate composite survival and basin capture

3. Collective-load saturation law
Hypothesis:
Crowding or excitation density limits regime stabilization.

Test:
	•	build a mesoscopic activity density observable
	•	introduce nonlinear saturation feedback
	•	check whether metastable composites persist longer

Each law must:
	•	be expressible with ≤ 2 new scalar parameters
	•	operate on coarse observables only
	•	preserve locality of the base architecture
	•	remain bounded and reversible

⸻

Pilot structure

For each candidate law:
	•	representatives:
	•	clustered composite anchor
	•	phase-ordered symmetric triad
	•	counter-propagating corridor
	•	conditions:
	•	null
	•	very weak regulation
	•	weak regulation

Total minimal pilot:
9 runs per law class

Refinement:
Only if a run shows simultaneous improvement in:
	•	composite lifetime
	•	or binding persistence
	•	or basin residence

⸻

Positive detection criteria

Promote only if all hold:
	•	persistence gain over null
	•	ordering survives 12 → 24 refinement
	•	effect is selective (not uniform across cases)
	•	regulation signal remains bounded
	•	no trivial global damping explains the result

Label outcome:
	•	candidate effective organizing law

⸻

Negative freeze rule

Freeze a law family negative if:
	•	no persistence improvement in all base runs
	•	refinement destroys any observed ordering
	•	improvement correlates only with trivial energy damping
	•	parameter sensitivity is extreme

⸻

Interpretation boundary

Even a positive Stage 20 result implies only:

emergence of a collective regulatory dynamic

It does not yet imply:
	•	geometry
	•	spacetime
	•	fundamental interaction
	•	binding mechanism

Those remain later-stage interpretations.

⸻

Structural purpose of Stage 20

This layer asks:

Can long-time organization be described
by a simple emergent rule?

If yes → Stage 21 can study stability classes.
If no → architecture may require qualitatively new degrees of freedom.
