Abstract

We report a structured numerical feasibility program investigating whether
reproducible emergence phenomena can stabilize inside a frozen branch-local
cochain-Laplacian hierarchy defined on discrete graph domains.

The study proceeds through a sequence of bounded diagnostic phases testing,
under controlled refinement hierarchies and matched altered-connectivity
controls, the stability of propagation bands, temporal ordering signals,
causal influence structures, and proto-geometric distance surrogates.

All telemetry definitions, initialization rules, and operator constructions
are frozen across phases to ensure invariant measurement conditions.
Results are therefore interpreted strictly as internal consistency and
reproducibility indicators rather than as claims of geometric or physical
correspondence.

Across the investigated refinement regimes we observe:

(1) bounded propagation scaling trends,
(2) stable event-ordering consistency scores,
(3) closure of influence graphs into acyclic causal structures, and
(4) refinement-coherent distance-surrogate indicators.

These findings support the feasibility of a reproducible emergence ladder
within the tested frozen branch regime.

No continuum limit, manifold reconstruction, or field-theoretic ontology is
asserted. The work is positioned as a disciplined numerical exploration of
whether structured macroscopic descriptors can arise from constrained
operator-driven dynamics.

The program establishes a foundation for future coarse-graining,
universality, and scaling-law investigations.
