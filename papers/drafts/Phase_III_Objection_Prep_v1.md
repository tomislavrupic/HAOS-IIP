# Phase III Objection Prep v1

## Objection 1

If braid-like exchange does not generalize beyond clustered seeds, why keep it in the paper at all?

Response:
Because Phase III is not only a negative story. The clustered branch contains a real local texture split that survives reduction, closure, and mechanism-isolation passes. The paper keeps braid only at the correct level: as a clustered texture descriptor, not as a family-wide intrinsic mechanism.

## Objection 2

If the phase diagram closes on the smeared side, does the braid result disappear entirely?

Response:
No. The braid read remains part of the local clustered phenomenology, but it does not survive as a stable closed phase. The paper therefore distinguishes descriptive presence from closed structural status.

## Objection 3

Why claim a valid effective model if the braid is not general?

Response:
The effective reduction is explicitly clustered-sector bounded. Its validity is local to the tested anchor lattice and does not overturn the non-generality result. The paper separates local effective validity from family-wide mechanism claims.

## Objection 4

Why is phase corridor called primary if geometry overlap and operator coupling also matter?

Response:
Because only phase flattening kills the clustered braid read already at weak ablation. Geometry-overlap weakening and `0 <-> 1` operator cross-coupling suppression collapse the braid only at moderate ablation, so they are retained as secondary supports rather than primary controls.

## Objection 5

Could grade transfer still be primary under a different implementation?

Response:
Not on this frozen branch. The recorded claim is narrower: grade transfer does not emerge as the primary control variable on the frozen branch under the bounded surrogate that preserves operator-class discipline. Reopening that question requires a new branch with explicit structural changes.
