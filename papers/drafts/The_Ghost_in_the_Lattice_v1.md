## 1. Introduction: The Search for a Unified Digital Fabric

The HAOS-IIP Research Stack represents a pivot toward architectural unity. By seeking the discrete analogues of geometry and vectors within this lattice, the researchers are testing a hypothesis: that a single interaction kernel can support scalar, vector, and eventually spinor sectors merely through different operator lifts.

## 2. The "One Kernel" Philosophy: Elegance through Minimalist Architecture

The HAOS-IIP architecture is predicated on the idea that physical diversity should arise from operator transformations rather than heterogeneous substrates. At the heart of the system is a weighted interaction kernel `A_ij` acting on nodes `x_i` with a defined kernel width `epsilon_k`. This kernel defines the weighted interaction fabric that underlies all subsequent physical structures.

> same substrate + same kernel + different operator lifts

Under this paradigm, the research stack bifurcates into distinct levels:

- `L0` (Node Sector): A node operator derived from the kernel that produces scalar or geometry-like structures.
- `L1` (Edge Sector): A Hodge-type operator acting on oriented edge fields to test for vector-sector emergence.
- `DH` (Dirac Lift): A planned spinorial operator for future fermion-sector investigations.

By deriving both `L0` and `L1` from the same weighted substrate, the architecture ensures that geometry and gauge-like behavior are mathematically unified at the most fundamental level of the graph.

## 3. The Falsification of Scalar Leakage: Establishing a Unique Vector Sector

In discrete physics, a recurring falsification risk is that any apparent vector behavior is merely a shadow or leakage of underlying scalar data. If the low-energy spectrum of a vector operator is entirely composed of gradients of scalar functions, the model has failed to find a unique vector sector.

To address this, the researchers utilized the Hodge Laplacian

`L1 = d0 d0* + d1* d1`

which allows for a formal three-part decomposition of the edge space `C1`:

`C1 = im d0 (+) H1 (+) im d1*`

Here, `im d0` represents exact (scalar-derived) components, `im d1*` represents coexact components, and `H1` represents harmonic modes.

The researchers established a baseline using the `L0` operator, which yielded scalar benchmarks of `lambda_1 ~= 2.410`, `lambda_2 ~= 2.415`, and `lambda_3 ~= 2.422`. When testing `L1` on an open box, they found total scalar dominance:

- Open Box (`n = 5`): the lowest eigenvalues (`lambda ~= 0.326`) showed an exact fraction of `1.000` and a divergence-free fraction of `0.000`.

This leakage was only overcome by shifting to a periodic torus `T^3_n`, where the results inverted. On the torus at trivial flux (`m = 0`), the lowest modes became pure harmonic cycles with a `1.000` divergence-free fraction. This confirmed that the `L1` operator captures a genuine, non-scalar branch of information that is not merely inherited from `L0`.

## 4. Topological Necessity and the Nontrivial Harmonic Sector

The transition from an open box to a discrete 3-torus `T^3_n` was not merely a change in boundary conditions; it was a topological requirement for the existence of the vector sector. On a contractible open box, the harmonic sector `H1` is mathematically trivial, meaning any low-energy vector behavior is forced to be a scalar gradient.

By identifying the opposite faces of the cubic box to form a torus, the researchers introduced the nontrivial topology necessary for loops of information to persist. As the source notes:

> This periodic identification... introduces a nontrivial low harmonic sector on edge space that is absent on the open box.

This topological locking is what allows the vector branch to survive as a distinct mathematical entity, independent of the scalar-dominated background of the open graph.

## 5. Kinematics without Dynamics: The Response to Ghostly Flux

To further stress-test the architecture, the researchers performed a periodic twisted experiment. They introduced a background `U(1)` connection in a discrete Landau gauge, essentially subjecting the lattice to a mathematical flux `m`.

The results of the flux scan provided the most compelling evidence for a responsive vector branch. For a lattice size of `n = 5`, as the flux quantum `m` was increased from `0` to `1`, the zero modes, the lowest energy states, began to lift. Specifically:

- the eigenvalue `lambda` lifted from `0.000` to `0.013`
- the mode remained `0.975` divergence-free

This result demonstrates kinematics without dynamics. The system exhibits holonomy and responds to a connection `U_ij`, yet it lacks the dynamical action, such as a Maxwell `F^2` term, required for a full gauge theory. The mathematical structure is prepared for the field, demonstrating an inherent sensitivity to holonomy even in the absence of a defined energy for the plaquettes.

## 6. The Epistemological Honesty of the Non-Photon

Despite the success of the `L1` tests, the HAOS-IIP researchers maintain a rigorous intellectual distance from over-hyped theory-of-everything claims. They explicitly state that their current findings do not represent an emergent photon or a complete Maxwell theory.

The architecture currently lacks three vital pillars:

1. Dynamical action: there is no action for the link variables `U`.
2. Plaquette energy: the system does not yet possess a term playing the role of electromagnetic field strength `F^2`.
3. Propagating band: the numerics have not yet isolated a purely coexact propagating band; instead, they see a flux-deformed harmonic sector.

By acknowledging that they have isolated a vector-like branch rather than a photon, the researchers ground their work in the verifiable reality of operator-level evidence. This honesty differentiates the HAOS-IIP stack from more speculative attempts to derive physics from graphs, focusing instead on the concrete stability of the Hodge-sector decomposition.
