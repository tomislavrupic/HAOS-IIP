\section*{Appendix C0: No-Distance Combinatorial Austerity Line}

Appendix C0 collects the pure no-distance combinatorial branch from Stages C0.1 through C0.8 without rewriting the original Phase I or Phase II files. The line begins as a metric-removal control on an explicit edge-graph branch, then extends through degree-spectrum protection, incidence-noise robustness, controlled motif injection, protected sequencing, forced mismatch accumulation, kernel-inconsistency forcing, and finally a weaker bounded-path kernel with bi-root and multi-layer motif injection. Throughout the series the graph size, representative encounter families, projected-transverse sector, and shell-weight measurement vocabulary remain fixed; only the combinatorial forcing and survivor-kernel structure are changed.

The combined numerical boundary is now sharp. C0.6 establishes reproducible positive mismatch accumulation and protected ordering. C0.7 shows that the original adjacency versus graph-shell alternation is still too rigid to produce explicit kernel-update inconsistency. C0.8 softens that boundary: the weaker bounded-path kernel opens explicit adjacency-versus-kernel inconsistency in five of nine runs, and the hybrid noise plus motif forcing protocol produces protected ordered persistence in two of nine runs while preserving the same coarse family labels (\texttt{trapped\_local}, \texttt{smeared\_transfer}, and \texttt{split\_channel\_exchange}) across the full matrix.

The honest sentence to carry forward is:
\begin{quote}
\emph{The weaker bounded-path kernel can generate explicit adjacency-versus-kernel inconsistency on the no-distance branch, especially under combined noise+motif forcing, but protected irreversibility remains too sparse to justify Phase II as a generally supported claim yet.}
\end{quote}

This appendix therefore gives a clean numerical challenge to any automatic non-functoriality claim for time emergence. The present no-distance branch supports incidence-forced protected sequencing and can be pushed into explicit inconsistency, but it does not yet support a broad claim that irreversibility follows automatically from that inconsistency on the current combinatorial control family.
