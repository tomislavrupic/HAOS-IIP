\section*{Appendix C0: No-Distance Combinatorial Austerity Line (Paper 25.1 support appendix)}

Appendix C0 collects the no-distance combinatorial branch from Stages C0.1 through C0.14 without rewriting the original Phase I, Phase II, or Phase III files. It is reserved here as the support appendix for Paper 25.1. The line begins as a metric-removal control on an explicit edge-graph branch, then extends through degree-spectrum protection, incidence-noise robustness, controlled motif injection, protected sequencing, forced mismatch accumulation, kernel-inconsistency forcing, weaker bounded-path survivor maps, tri-root motif forcing, harmonic-address compatibility, resonance and detuning scans, boundary refinement, and finally a locking-mechanism null test. Throughout the series the graph size, representative encounter families, projected-transverse sector, and shell-weight measurement vocabulary remain fixed within each sub-branch; only the combinatorial forcing, survivor-kernel structure, or harmonic-address rule is changed.

The combined numerical boundary is now broader and sharper. C0.6 establishes reproducible positive mismatch accumulation and protected ordering. C0.7 shows that the original adjacency versus graph-shell alternation is still too rigid to produce explicit kernel-update inconsistency. C0.8 softens that boundary: the weaker bounded-path kernel opens explicit adjacency-versus-kernel inconsistency in five of nine runs, and the hybrid noise plus motif forcing protocol produces protected ordered persistence in two of nine runs while preserving the same coarse family labels (\texttt{trapped\_local}, \texttt{smeared\_transfer}, and \texttt{split\_channel\_exchange}) across the full matrix. C0.9 then fails to scale that partial positive. The later harmonic-address branch C0.10--C0.14 opens a weak topology-selection signal on the clustered DK seed, resolves a narrow braid-to-smear detuning transition near $\delta \approx 0.75$, and then closes negatively: no tested bounded latch converts selection into locking.

The honest sentence to carry forward is:
\begin{quote}
\emph{The C0 line supports graph-structural class survival, reproducible incidence-driven mismatch accumulation, partial kernel inconsistency under softer survivor maps, and weak harmonic-address topology selection, but it does not yet support broad irreversibility scaling or topology locking on the no-distance control branch.}
\end{quote}

This appendix therefore gives a clean numerical challenge to any automatic non-functoriality claim for time emergence while also separating selection from locking. The present no-distance branch supports incidence-forced protected sequencing and can be pushed into explicit inconsistency, but it does not yet support a broad claim that irreversibility follows automatically from that inconsistency on the current combinatorial control family. Nor does the later harmonic-address line support the stronger claim that selected topology locks itself into a bounded exchange regime.
