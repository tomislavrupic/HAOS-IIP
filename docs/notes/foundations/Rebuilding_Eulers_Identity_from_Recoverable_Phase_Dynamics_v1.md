# Rebuilding Euler's Identity from Recoverable Phase Dynamics

A short HAOS/IIP note

## Abstract

Euler's identity

$$
e^{i\theta}=\cos\theta+i\sin\theta
$$

is usually presented as a result of complex analysis. In the HAOS/IIP framework it can be interpreted more structurally: as the minimal spectral law governing norm-preserving recoverable rotation in a finite interaction system. In this note we show how the appearance of the imaginary unit and Euler's exponential form arise naturally once recoverable phase dynamics are required.

## 1. Recoverable Interaction

In IIP the primitive object is not a number but an interaction state that evolves while remaining recoverable under repeated interaction.

Let a state be represented by a minimal two-component vector

$$
\Psi(\theta)=
\begin{pmatrix}
x(\theta)\\
y(\theta)
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Recoverability requires the state norm to remain invariant:

$$
x^2+y^2=\text{const}.
$$

Thus the evolution must lie on a unit orbit in state space.

Geometrically this is a circle.

## 2. The Minimal Generator of Recoverable Rotation

A smooth evolution that preserves the norm must be generated by an antisymmetric operator. The minimal form is

$$
J=
\begin{pmatrix}
0 & -1\\
1 & 0
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

The evolution equation becomes

$$
\frac{d\Psi}{d\theta}=J\Psi .
$$

This operator has the key property

$$
J^2=-I.
$$

The algebraic role normally played by the imaginary unit $i$ is therefore played by the rotation generator $J$.

In this sense the imaginary unit is not a mysterious number but the minimal operator generating recoverable phase rotation.

## 3. Exponentiation and Spectral Evolution

The solution of the evolution equation is given by the operator exponential

$$
\Psi(\theta)=e^{\theta J}\Psi(0).
$$

Expanding the exponential,

$$
e^{\theta J}
=
I+\theta J+\frac{\theta^2}{2!}J^2+\frac{\theta^3}{3!}J^3+\dots
$$

and using $J^2=-I$, the series separates into even and odd parts:

$$
e^{\theta J}
=
I\cos\theta + J\sin\theta .
$$

This is the minimal spectral decomposition of recoverable rotation.

## 4. Emergence of Euler's Identity

If we identify the operator $J$ with the imaginary unit $i$,

$$
J \leftrightarrow i,
$$

the expression becomes

$$
e^{i\theta}=\cos\theta+i\sin\theta.
$$

Euler's identity therefore appears as the compressed algebraic representation of recoverable cyclic dynamics.

## 7. Conclusion

Euler's identity can be reconstructed without assuming complex numbers as primitive objects. Instead it emerges from a single structural requirement:

recoverable cyclic interaction must preserve norm.

The minimal generator of such evolution squares to $-1$, and its exponential produces the cosine-sine decomposition known as Euler's formula.

In HAOS/IIP terms:

Euler's identity is the spectral law of norm-preserving recoverable rotation.

It is therefore not merely an abstract algebraic curiosity but the compressed operator expression of cyclic persistence in recoverable interaction dynamics.
