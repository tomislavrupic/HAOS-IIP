## Practical rule for the repo

When a document speaks about:

- what is real
- what survives perturbation
- what interaction makes possible
- what contraction preserves
- what counts as invariant residue

it is a candidate core source.

When a document speaks about:

- kernels
- operators
- spectra
- geometry recovery
- numerical validation
- sector architecture

it is a model or experiment source unless explicitly promoted into canon under the axiom boundary.

### Classification summary

This bundle is not one thing.

It contains three different layers:

#### Layer 1 - Core-adjacent formalization

These parts are strongly aligned with the frozen core and may be used as explanatory support:

- the constraint-first diagram specification
- the allowed / forbidden axis rules
- the primitive elements:
  - carrier
  - constraint
  - interaction
- the operational quantities:
  - coherence
  - perturbation magnitude
  - recoverability
  - boundedness
- the insistence on hostile readability, reproducibility, and anti-metaphorical structure

Classification:

- `core-adjacent formalization`

Why:

- these sections operationalize the core without replacing it
- they mostly preserve the audit boundary
- they are presentation and formalization tools, not ontology inflation

#### Layer 2 - Model and application layer

These parts do not belong to the frozen core:

- periodic-table regional mapping
- iron-peak and nucleosynthesis interpretation
- time-arrow commentary
- quantum-interpretation critique
- AI overlay
- AI persistence metrics
- scaling-law appendix

Classification:

- `model / application layer`

Why:

- these are domain-specific realizations or explanatory overlays
- they may be good, bad, strong, weak, or incomplete, but they are not the axioms
- they should sit beneath the core and be tested like any other realization branch

#### Layer 3 - Implementation layer

These parts are implementation or methods material:

- persistence-under-interaction pseudocode
- recovery-cost formulas
- perturbation / recovery simulation flow

Classification:

- `implementation layer`

Why:

- they are useful as research tooling
- they do not define the framework itself

### Main boundary result

The most core-safe sentence family in the pasted bundle is the one that says, in effect:

- coherence must survive perturbation
- only operational axes are allowed
- diagrams must survive hostile interpretation
- symbolic smoothness or narrative geometry is not evidence

That is consistent with the frozen core.

The least core-safe parts are the places where the bundle moves from audit language into explanatory overreach, especially:

- “time is how that cascade feels from inside”
- “constraints can move matter without forces”
- “many quantum interpretations are coordinate mistakes”
- “iron is the true center”

These may be valuable as provocative model-layer claims, but they are not core truths and should not be written into canon without a separate evidential program.

### Clean repo placement rule for this bundle

If retained, the bundle should be split as follows:

- diagram-spec and axis-discipline material:
  - `docs/notes/foundations/` or a future canon-adjacent presentation guide
- primitive vocabulary and operational quantities:
  - `docs/notes/foundations/`

  - `docs/notes/applications/` or papers / appendices
- pseudocode and measurement protocols:
  - `numerics/` notes, methods appendices, or experimental docs

### Final verdict on the pasted bundle

The bundle does not contaminate the HAOS-IIP core if its layers are separated.

Its best parts strengthen the external presentation of the core.

Its strongest risks come from letting applied interpretations or rhetorical conclusions masquerade as axioms.
